Hi, I’m **Shubham Kumar**.

I’m interested in mathematics, machine learning, and the ideas behind modern generative AI. This site is my public learning notebook: a place where I turn questions, experiments, and difficult concepts into explanations I would want to read myself.

## What I write about

- **Mathematics:** linear algebra, probability, information theory, and optimization.
- **Machine learning:** classical models, deep-learning foundations, and training practice.
- **Generative AI:** variational inference, VAEs, diffusion models, and the connections between them.

Most articles begin with a genuine doubt. I work from intuition to equations, test the idea with examples, and preserve the reasoning that made the concept click. The goal is not to sound like a textbook; it is to make complicated ideas easier to think about.

## Why this blog exists

Writing exposes gaps that passive reading can hide. Publishing those notes forces me to make the argument clearer, verify the details, and leave behind something useful for the next person asking the same question.

If an article helps you—or if you notice something that can be improved—I’d be glad to hear from you.
